## Creating Your First Trip

### Step 1: Name your destination

From the home screen, tap the **+** button or **Plan a New Trip**.

Type your destination — a city, region, or country works. Tap the suggestion that matches where you're going.

### Step 2: Set your dates and budget

- Choose your **start** and **end** dates
- Set a **total budget** (optional but recommended — it helps the AI give more relevant suggestions)
- Choose your **trip type**: solo, couple, family, group, or business

### Step 3: Tell us your preferences

- **Pace:** Relaxed (fewer activities, more breathing room), Moderate, or Packed (maximum sights)
- **Interests:** Food, temples, street art, hiking, nightlife, museums — pick as many as you like
- **Dietary needs:** Vegetarian, vegan, halal, gluten-free, or any combination

Tap **Generate My Trip** and EasyTrip will build a full day-by-day itinerary using AI, typically in 15–30 seconds.

### What you get

Once generated, your trip includes:
- A day-by-day plan with timed activities
- Walking and transport times between each stop
- Restaurant recommendations for breakfast, lunch, and dinner
- Estimated costs per activity
- A weather preview for your dates

> **Note:** If you're planning somewhere less commonly visited, you'll see a banner letting you know our data may be less detailed. The itinerary will still work, but verify specific details locally.

## The Daily Planner

The Daily Planner is the heart of EasyTrip. It shows your day as an ordered checklist, with everything timed and routed.

### Navigating your days

Swipe left and right (or tap the day tabs at the top) to move between days. Each day shows a title, a short summary, and the weather forecast.

### Completing tasks

Tap the circle next to any activity to mark it done. A progress bar at the top of the day shows how much of the day you've completed. Completed tasks stay visible but greyed out — so you can still reference them.

### Reordering activities

Long-press any activity and drag it to a new position. The plan adapts — travel times update automatically.

### Adding your own activities

> Requires Voyager or Nomad Pro.

Tap the **+** button at the bottom of any day to add a custom activity. Give it a title, time, category, and estimated cost. Custom activities behave identically to AI-generated ones.

### Viewing a place

Tap any activity to see its full detail:
- Photos
- Opening hours (sourced from Google — verify on arrival for unusual hours)
- Entry fee
- Address and directions
- Peak hour warning (if the venue gets very busy at certain times)

From the place detail, tap **Get Directions** to open routing in EasyTrip's transport screen, or tap the map pin to view the location.

### Regenerating a day

Not happy with a day? Tap the **⟳** icon on any day header to regenerate just that day (Voyager+). The rest of your trip stays the same.

## Finding Restaurants and Venues

### The Food & Dining screen

Tap **Food** in the bottom navigation bar. EasyTrip shows you restaurants and cafés near your current location (or your destination if you're planning ahead).

### Filtering results

Use the filter bar to narrow down by:
- **Cuisine type** (Japanese, Italian, street food, etc.)
- **Dietary requirements** (vegan, halal, vegetarian, gluten-free)
- **Budget** (budget / mid-range / upscale)

### Local dishes

At the top of the Food screen, tap **Local Dishes** to see what the area is known for — helpful for knowing what to order when you get there.

### Best food areas

Tap **Areas** to see which neighbourhoods are best for eating in your destination. Useful if you want to wander rather than plan.

### Venue cards

Each venue card shows:
- Google rating and price level (£ to ££££)
- Dietary tags
- Distance and travel time from your current position
- Opening hours summary
- A **Trending** badge if the venue is currently popular on social media (Nomad Pro)

Tap a venue to see full details, photos, and the option to add it to your trip.

## Using the Translator

### Text translation

Tap **Translator** in the navigation. Type or paste any text — EasyTrip detects the language automatically and translates it to your chosen language.

Tap the swap button to flip languages.

### Camera translation (Voyager+)

Tap the **camera icon** to point your camera at a menu, sign, or notice. EasyTrip reads the text using Google's OCR technology and translates it on-screen. Translated text is overlaid on the image.

Works best in good lighting. Supports printed text in Latin, Japanese, Korean, Chinese, Arabic, Cyrillic, and most other scripts.

### Phrasebook

Tap **Phrasebook** to browse useful phrases in the local language. Phrases are grouped by category:

- Greetings
- Transport
- Food & ordering
- Emergency
- Shopping
- Accommodation

### Pronunciation audio (Voyager+)

Tap the **speaker icon** next to any phrase to hear it spoken aloud in the local language by a native voice.

### Saving phrases

Tap the **bookmark icon** on any phrase to save it to your personal phrase library. Saved phrases are available offline (Voyager+).

## Budget Tracking

### Setting your budget

Open your trip, tap the **Budget** tab. If you set a budget when creating the trip, it'll already be there. You can edit the total and set category allocations (food, transport, accommodation, activities, other).

### Logging expenses

Tap the **+ Expense** button to log a purchase.

- Enter the **amount** in whatever currency you paid
- EasyTrip converts it to your trip currency automatically using live exchange rates
- Choose a **category**
- Optionally add a description or link it to a venue

Expenses can be logged offline and will sync when you reconnect.

### Tracking your spend

The budget overview shows:
- Spending by category as a visual breakdown
- Total spent vs. remaining budget
- A day-by-day estimated cost from your itinerary to help you plan ahead

### Currency

EasyTrip stores all expenses in your trip's base currency. Exchange rates update every hour. The rate used for each expense is shown in the detail view.

## Social Intelligence (Pro)

> Requires Nomad Pro subscription.

Social Intelligence is EasyTrip's live feed of what real people — travellers, locals, influencers — are posting about your destination right now.

### What it shows

The Social Intelligence screen shows posts from YouTube, Reddit, Twitter/X, and travel blogs, scored by how trendy and relevant they are. Each post shows:

- The creator's name and follower count
- A short excerpt (max 50 words) and a link to the original
- The **Trend Score** (0–100) — how much momentum this place or post has right now
- The venue it's about, if one was detected

### Filtering the feed

Use the filter bar to show only food-related posts, landmark posts, or all. Sort by trend score (default) or by most recent.

### Trending destinations

Tap **Trending** to see which cities are getting the most social media attention this week. Good for spontaneous trip inspiration.

### Celebrity and influencer picks

Tap **Celeb Picks** to filter to posts from verified accounts and well-known travel creators.

### On venue cards

Wherever you see a venue in EasyTrip, you'll see a **Trending** badge and the current trend score if social media activity has been detected for that place. Tap the badge to read the posts.

> Social Intelligence data is updated every 30–60 minutes. Coverage is strongest for major cities.

## AI Assistant (Pro)

> Requires Nomad Pro subscription.

The AI Assistant is a conversational travel advisor that knows your itinerary and can help you adjust, improve, or answer questions about your trip.

### Opening the assistant

Tap the **AI** tab in the navigation, or tap the chat bubble icon on any trip screen.

### What you can ask

The assistant knows your full itinerary — destinations, dates, activities, budget, and preferences. Examples:

- "Can you swap Day 2's morning to something less touristy?"
- "What's the best way to get from Shinjuku to Shibuya on a Sunday?"
- "Is there a night market I should add to Day 4?"
- "I'm vegetarian and can't find food on Day 3 evening — can you suggest somewhere?"
- "What should I know about tipping in Japan?"

### Context

The assistant has full context of your trip from the start of each conversation. You don't need to re-explain your itinerary.

Conversation history is saved per trip. To start fresh, tap **Clear conversation** in the menu.

### Limits

The assistant is for trip-related questions. It won't book things for you or access live transport APIs directly — use the Transport screen for real-time routing.

## Themes and Customisation

> Themes require Voyager or Nomad Pro. The default theme (Light/Dark) is free.

### Changing your theme

Go to **Settings → Theme** and choose from:

- **Light / Dark** — Clean, minimal. Follows your phone's system setting.
- **Aurora Dark** — Deep night sky with teal and purple accents.
- **Warm Sand** — Warm earthy tones with terracotta and olive.
- **Electric** — High-contrast dark with neon lime and hot pink.

Theme changes take effect instantly across the whole app.

### Customising category colours (Voyager+)

Each theme has default colours for activity categories (food, landmarks, transport, etc.). You can override these per theme.

Go to **Settings → Theme → Customise Colours** and tap any category to choose your own colour.

### Other settings

- **Currency:** Change your default display currency anytime in Settings
- **Language:** Change the app interface language
- **Notifications:** Toggle trip reminders and day-start alerts

## Offline Mode

> Offline access requires Voyager or Nomad Pro.

### What works offline

Once you open a trip with an internet connection, EasyTrip saves the following to your device:

- Full itinerary (all days and tasks)
- Venue details and photos (thumbnails) for every activity in the trip
- Saved phrasebook entries and audio pronunciation files
- Your logged expenses
- Exchange rates (refreshed every 6 hours when online)

### What requires internet

- Generating or regenerating an itinerary
- Live restaurant search
- Text and camera translation (beyond saved phrases)
- Social Intelligence feed
- AI Assistant
- Real-time transport routing

### How to prepare for offline use

1. Open your trip and browse through your itinerary while connected
2. Tap into a few venue detail pages — photos are cached as you view them
3. In Translator → Phrasebook, browse through the language you need

EasyTrip caches what you've viewed. The more you browse while connected, the more will be available offline.

### Syncing changes

Any changes you make offline (completing tasks, logging expenses, reordering activities) are saved locally and synced to the server automatically when you reconnect.

## Tips

**Before you travel:**
- Generate your itinerary at least a day before you go so everything is cached offline
- Browse your venue detail pages to cache the photos and opening hours
- Download your phrasebook language pack in Translator settings

**On the ground:**
- Use the task checkboxes as you go — the progress bar is satisfying
- Log expenses as you spend — it takes 5 seconds and your end-of-trip summary will thank you
- Tap **Get Directions** on any task to get live routing from your current GPS location

**If something changes:**
- Drag tasks to reorder your day
- Use the regenerate button on any day if your plans change significantly
- Ask the AI Assistant (Pro) for quick replanning suggestions
